About me.

I have a Master's in Counselling Psychology from the University of Bristol and a PhD in Cognitive Neuroscience from Paris. Before working clinically, I spent years as a researcher studying emotion using brain imaging and eye-tracking, across Belgium, Taiwan, and Germany, and presenting that work internationally. That research background changes how I work. I'm not applying a model to your experience. I understand how emotion is processed, how the brain builds patterns, and how behaviour locks those patterns in place. When we work together, that's what we're actually working with. I've also lived this. Five countries, four languages, the particular discipline and failure that comes with building a life across cultures. I know what it costs, and I know what becomes possible when someone finally understands their own pattern clearly. No discrimination, no judgement. What happens in the room stays in the room.

Brain. Behaviour. Emotion.

That's the triangle that holds. Brain is the why: cognition, pattern, neuroscience. Emotion is the what: felt experience, the body's signal. Behaviour is the how: what you actually do in the world. None of the three works alone, and most approaches only touch one or two. This is where we work differently.
